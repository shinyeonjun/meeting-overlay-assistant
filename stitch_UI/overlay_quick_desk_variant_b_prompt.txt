Create one desktop-first floating overlay panel for a Korean meeting assistant.
Core user: a Korean office worker who is already in a live meeting and wants the smallest possible side panel that only surfaces urgent questions.
Primary action: glance at open questions instantly and jump out to a bigger workspace only if needed.
Visual direction: ultra-minimal, dense, disciplined, almost like a command palette mixed with a live meeting monitor.
Color system: deep graphite background, slightly brighter surfaces, one cool blue accent, one mint live accent, very bright text.
Background treatment: plain dark background only.
Surface treatment: flat compact cards, thin borders, very low shadow, no decorative blur.

Must include:
- a tiny live status strip with a green live indicator, timer, and one-line topic
- a dominant "열린 질문" list with 4 to 5 short Korean question cards
- one tiny secondary section for "방금 나온 질문"
- one compact bottom action row with "웹", "리포트", "종료"
- Korean UI copy

Important constraints:
- narrow floating panel only, around 320 to 360px wide
- this must feel lighter and more minimal than a workspace
- no tabs
- no forms
- no large hero header
- no analytics, no metrics cards, no equal-weight multi-column layout
- questions must take at least 70 percent of the visual attention

This is NOT a dashboard, NOT an admin console, NOT a kanban board, and NOT a mini SaaS app.
Avoid decorative gradients, glossy glass panels, oversized section titles, and noisy management UI.
Generate exactly one screen draft.
